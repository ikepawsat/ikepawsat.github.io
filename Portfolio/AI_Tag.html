Simply put, I saw a creative attempt at an AI Tag game and wanted to give it a shot myself. Before this, I had never made a truly successful AI, let alone two that could learn off of eachother. Attempt 1

Having multiple scripts can be confusing, I quickily learned that I accidentally made the chaser get points for being further away so it found a way to glitch through the map. I fixed this and moved on.

Attempt 2

The AIs began to work really well even after a short amount of training; however, I was not happy with them because they would run into walls or get confused why they could not go forward (because of a wall) because they could not see them. To solve this, I added ray tracing to each of them, pointing up,down, right, and left, to give them environmental information.

Attempt 3

I may have overloaded them with this new information, after about 1300 runs they seem to get worse and get stuck doing stupid things. To fix this I decided to add what I like to call a "reset". To do this, I take the average of 1000 runs, and on each run after I start to see if that rounds reward is less than 90% of the average. If it is, I reset it to the max reward neural network which I have saved in the program when each new max occurs.
